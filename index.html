Story

Starfish, also known as sea stars, are marine animals that are star-shaped and come in a variety of colors and sizes. Despite their name, they are not actually fish. Starfish have five arms and can regenerate lost ones. They feed mostly on shellfish. The Lone Starfish in our story was discovered on a quiet beach, surrounded by clear water and white sand.

Facts About Starfish

  • Starfish are not actually fish they are echinoderms, related to sea urchins and sand dollars.
  • They usually have five arms, but some species can have as many as forty!
  • Starfish can regenerate lost arms, and in some cases, an entire new starfish can grow from a single arm.
  • They do not have brains or blood. Instead, they use filtered seawater to pump nutrients through their bodies.
  • Starfish have eyes at the tip of each arm that help them sense light and dark.
